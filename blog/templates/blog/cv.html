Books

  • Debunking Delusions tells the story of how the Treatment Action Campaign struggled against state-supported AIDS denialism and quackery in South Africa. You can buy it from Amazon. Since it is now out-of-print you are welcome to download this nearly-final version before typesetting as a PDF.
  • Witness to AIDS by Edwin Cameron was the joint winner, with Adam Levin's AIDSafari, of the 2006 Sunday Times/Alan Paton Prize, South Africa's premier literary award for non-fiction. I co-authored two chapters.

Websites

  • GroundUp reports human rights stories from many places in South Africa. (Founder and editor of GroundUp.)
  • Quackdown exposed nonsense health claims. It is no longer updated.
  • Simhub: Home of some of the simulation software I've written.
  • TB Online is a website reporting the latest news and activism related to tuberculosis. In 2014 I handed maintenance of the site over to the Treatment Action Group.

Software

  • FastSTI: simulates sexually transmitted infection epidemics
  • Space travel calculator
  • Simulating evolution of non-lethal agonistic behaviour
  • Partner matching in simulations of sexually transmitted infection epidemics.
  • Graphical implementation of the Prisoner's Dilemna game that Robert Axelrod and Douglas Hofstadter wrote elegantly about in the 1980s.
